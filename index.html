Important Information

    • Location: Extends from the Arctic to the Southern Ocean, bordered by the Americas, Europe, and Africa.
    • Depth and Features: Average depth of 3,646 meters; deepest point is the Puerto Rico Trench.
    • Climate Influence: Significantly impacts global climate, notably through the Gulf Stream.
    • Marine Life: Rich in biodiversity, home to species like the blue whale.
    • Economic Importance: Crucial for international trade, with major ports and resources like fish and oil.
    • Historical Significance: Central to the Age of Discovery and the Transatlantic Slave Trade.
    • Environmental Challenges: Facing issues like pollution, overfishing, and climate change impacts.
    • Scientific Research: A key site for studies in oceanography, marine biology, and climate science.
    • Cultural Significance: Integral to the folklore and arts of coastal communities.
    • Recreational Activities: Popular for sailing, fishing, diving, and surfing.

      • The Atlantic Ocean extends from the Arctic Ocean in the north to the Southern Ocean in the south, lying between the Americas to the west and Europe and Africa to the east. It covers an area of about 106,460,000 square kilometers.

      The name "Atlantic" is derived from the mythical Greek god Atlas. It has been a vital route for international trade and exploration since the time of the ancient civilizations. The ocean was first mapped by the Greek philosopher Ptolemy in the 2nd century.

      The Atlantic hosts a diverse range of marine life, including numerous species of fish, mammals, and plants. It's particularly noted for its significant populations of whales, dolphins, sharks, and turtles. Coral reefs, such as the Great Bahama Bank, are important habitats for marine biodiversity.

Latest News on the Atlantic Ocean

2023 Atlantic Hurricane Season

The 2023 Atlantic hurricane season, officially ending on November 30, was marked by above-normal activity with record-warm Atlantic sea surface temperatures and a strong El Niño. It saw 20 named storms, ranking as the fourth highest number of named storms since 1950.

Fossil Fuel-Free Flight Across the Atlantic

In a groundbreaking achievement for sustainable aviation, a Virgin Atlantic Boeing 787 flew from London to New York using only sustainable aviation fuel (SAF). This marks the first fossil fuel-free commercial flight across the Atlantic Ocean, signaling a major step in reducing the aviation industry's carbon footprint.

New Tropical Wave in the Atlantic

As the 2023 hurricane season neared its end, a new tropical wave was spotted in the eastern Atlantic Ocean. Observed five days before the season's close, weather analysts were closely monitoring this development.

Threat to North Atlantic Currents

Climate change poses a significant threat to the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC), a crucial part of the Earth’s global ocean circulation. The AMOC, encompassing a network of currents within the Atlantic Ocean, is vital for climate and marine ecosystems regulation.

Gargantuan Antarctic Iceberg Drifting

An immense Antarctic iceberg, five times the size of New York City and as thick as the Empire State Building, has begun drifting from the continental ice shelf towards the South Atlantic Ocean, presenting an intriguing yet concerning natural event.
